Bank & credit union financing

Land Loans

Land loans are the most common way to finance a raw land purchase. Unlike a traditional home mortgage, they're structured specifically around vacant or recreational property, and terms can vary depending on the lender, the size of the parcel, and how you plan to use the land.

Financing is available with competitive rates and as little as 10% down for qualified buyers.

Terms, rates, and down payment requirements vary by lender and buyer qualification. Consult your financial institution for current programs and eligibility.

Using a 401(k)

Many 401(k) plans allow participants to borrow against their vested balance, giving you a way to fund a land purchase without a separate loan application. Because rules vary by plan administrator, it's worth reviewing repayment terms and any tax implications with your plan provider before moving forward.

Retirement financing

Using an IRA

Certain self-directed IRAs permit real estate and land as a qualifying investment, allowing retirement funds to grow through land ownership. This route comes with specific IRS rules around use and distributions, so a financial advisor familiar with self-directed accounts can help you determine if it fits your retirement strategy.

HELOC Financing
Home equity

HELOC Financing

If you already own a home with built-up equity, a home equity line of credit can be a flexible way to finance a land purchase — often with a faster approval process than a new land loan. Terms and available credit depend on your home's equity and your lender's requirements.

Direct financing

Seller Financing

In select cases, Westshore Land Sales may be able to arrange financing directly, allowing qualified buyers to purchase land without going through a traditional lender. Ask a land consultant whether seller financing is available for the community you're interested in.

Cash Purchases

Paying cash is the simplest way to buy land — no loan approval and no financing contingencies, often with a faster path to closing. It's a popular option for buyers who want to move quickly or hold a property free and clear from day one.
